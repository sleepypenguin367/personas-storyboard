Personas & Storyboarding

Gasoline Pumping Machine

Sketch and Description

The interface is a gasoline pumping machine. The purpose is to let users fill gas themselves. The button is for making choices from the screen. The three large buttons below is for choosing the fuel type. The handle is for actually filling the gas.

Interview Observations

Questions for users:

  • Does the gas dispenser machine interface work as you expected?

  • What motives you to push the button instead of touching the screen?

  • Is there any actions you took that you think it is unnecessary while still delivering the same result?

  • Recall the first time you used the gas dispenser machine, how differently you use it between now and before?

  • What is the flow of the whole interaction? When did you discover the flow?

Observations:

  • All users started with pushing the button instead of touching the screen even though the screen is big enough to be considered as a touch screen. Users responded that choices are mapped with the button, and there also arrows on the answer pointing to the button. The low resolution of the screen also gives them a sense that itâ€™s old enough to not carrying these features.

  • Two users responded that when paying, the machine asked the type of card to pay with. They think that it is totally unnecessary since when they were using POS machine in other places such as grocery store, the POS machine can accept all card without distinguishing them before swiping.

  • All users agree that the pin pad is not covered. People can easily see when they are typing their pin. They had to cover them with their hands.

  • When people have their first gas station experience, they often do not know which side their fuel tank is on. They do not know whether to plug in first or choose their gas type first.

  • They also did not know that they can clipped the button instead of keeping pushing to continually adding fuel. They learned how to properly use the machine either by trying a few time or being taught by some experienced driver.

Empathy Map 1

Mina, a new driver who just got her first car and trying to add gasoline for the first time.

Mina is confused on how to use the gasoline machine. She did not know whether she plug in properly. She did not know which type of fuel and which side of tank she has. The pin pad is also not covered.

This represents the group of new drivers who just started using the interface. They may encounter problems on unclear instructions.

Empathy Map 2

Anna, a driver has beening driving for years.

Anna has no place to park because too many cars has the same sided tank. She does not know which gas station accept her desired card since there are so many gas stations.

This represents the group of experienced drivers. They know the process but some interface are making them frustrating.
